About

In the framework of the collaboration between the UdK and the WZB Berlin Social Science Center, we joined the research team “Waves of Regime Transformation” led by Dr. Vanessa Boese-Schlosser.

The following visualisations are based on a tree-ring diagram, in which each ring represents the country's democratic score according to the ERT V-Dem dataset (see About the Data). Allowing us to trace patterns in regime transformation, showing the waves of democratisation and autocratisation spanning from 1900 to 2025, reading from center outwards.

About the Data

We use the Episodes of Regime Transformation (ERT) dataset from V-Dem. Each country's democratic performance is measured using the Electoral democracy index, which assigns a value between 0 (Closed Autocracy) and 1 (Liberal Democracy). A score of 0 indicates the absence of free elections and political freedoms, while a score of 1 represents a fully functioning electoral democracy. The ERT dataset identifies sustained periods of political change in two directions: democratisation (liberalising autocracy and democratic deepening) and autocratisation (democratic regression and autocratic regression). This episodic view allows each ring to speak no only to a single year’s value, but to a larger pattern of democratic evolution it belongs to.

About the Graphic

Read from the centre outward, like the growth rings of a tree, each diagram traces a country's democratic development from 1900 to 2025. The line thickness reflects the Electoral democracy index: thicker lines represent lower scores (closer to 0, autocracy), while thinner lines represent higher scores (closer to 1, democracy). The line shape indicates periods of regime change. Straight segments represent years without a transformation episode, while undulating segments mark sustained episodes of democratisation or autocratisation.
